# STAR Interview Framework

## Overview
The STAR method is a structured approach to answering behavioural interview questions by describing a specific Situation, Task, Action, and Result.

## Components

### Situation
- Set the scene and give context
- Be specific about time, place, and circumstances
- Keep it concise — 2-3 sentences maximum
- Choose situations relevant to the role you're applying for

### Task
- Describe your specific responsibility or challenge
- Clarify what was expected of you
- Distinguish your role from the team's role
- Quantify the scope if possible (e.g., "managed a team of 5", "responsible for a £200k budget")

### Action
- This is the most important part — describe what YOU did
- Use "I" not "we" — interviewers want to know your individual contribution
- Be specific about your approach and reasoning
- Explain WHY you chose that action over alternatives
- Include technical details relevant to the role
- Show leadership, initiative, or problem-solving skills

### Result
- Quantify outcomes wherever possible (%, £, time saved, users impacted)
- Include both direct results and indirect benefits
- Mention what you learned from the experience
- If the result was negative, explain what you learned and what you'd do differently

## Common STAR Mistakes
1. Being too vague — "I worked on a project" tells the interviewer nothing
2. Focusing on "we" instead of "I" — the interviewer is hiring YOU
3. Skipping the Result — always close the loop with measurable outcomes
4. Choosing irrelevant situations — tailor your stories to the job description
5. Rambling — keep each STAR response to 2-3 minutes maximum

## Behavioural Question Patterns
- "Tell me about a time when..."
- "Describe a situation where..."
- "Give me an example of..."
- "How did you handle..."
- "What would you do if..."

## Sample Questions by Category

### Leadership
- Tell me about a time you led a project with competing priorities
- Describe a situation where you had to influence without authority
- Give an example of when you mentored someone

### Problem Solving
- Tell me about a complex technical problem you solved
- Describe a time when you had to make a decision with incomplete information
- How did you approach a situation where the requirements were unclear?

### Teamwork
- Tell me about a time you disagreed with a colleague
- Describe how you handled a conflict within your team
- Give an example of successful cross-functional collaboration

### Adaptability
- Tell me about a time you had to learn something quickly
- Describe a situation where your initial approach didn't work
- How did you handle a major change in project direction?
