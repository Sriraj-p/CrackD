# Competency Framework for Interview Assessment

## Overview
This framework defines the key competencies assessed during professional interviews across common graduate and early-career roles. Interviewers evaluate candidates against these dimensions to determine role fit.

## Universal Competencies (All Roles)

### Communication
- Clarity of thought and expression
- Ability to explain complex ideas simply
- Active listening and responsiveness
- Written communication quality (assessed via resume and follow-ups)
- Presentation skills

### Problem Solving
- Structured thinking and logical reasoning
- Breaking down complex problems into components
- Identifying root causes vs symptoms
- Creative and lateral thinking
- Data-driven decision making

### Adaptability
- Response to changing requirements
- Learning agility — how quickly can they pick up new tools/concepts?
- Comfort with ambiguity
- Resilience under pressure
- Growth mindset evidence

### Collaboration
- Teamwork and cross-functional experience
- Conflict resolution approach
- Giving and receiving feedback
- Stakeholder management
- Remote/distributed team experience

### Initiative & Ownership
- Self-directed projects and contributions
- Going beyond the minimum requirements
- Proactive problem identification
- Accountability for outcomes
- Evidence of leadership (formal or informal)

## Technical Role Competencies

### Software Engineering
- System design and architecture thinking
- Code quality and best practices
- Testing methodology and coverage
- Performance optimisation awareness
- Version control and CI/CD familiarity
- Debugging and troubleshooting approach
- API design and documentation

### Data Science / ML Engineering
- Statistical thinking and experimental design
- Model selection and evaluation methodology
- Data preprocessing and feature engineering
- MLOps and model deployment awareness
- Business problem framing
- Visualisation and storytelling with data
- Ethical AI considerations

### Product Management
- Customer empathy and user research
- Prioritisation frameworks (RICE, MoSCoW, etc.)
- Metrics definition and measurement
- Stakeholder alignment
- Technical fluency (not necessarily depth)
- Go-to-market thinking
- Roadmap planning and trade-off articulation

## Assessment Scoring Guide

### Level 1: No Evidence (0-20)
Candidate shows no relevant experience or awareness of the competency.

### Level 2: Basic Awareness (21-40)
Candidate understands the concept but has limited practical experience.

### Level 3: Developing (41-60)
Candidate has some practical experience, can discuss examples, but depth is limited.

### Level 4: Competent (61-80)
Candidate demonstrates solid experience with clear, specific examples and measurable outcomes.

### Level 5: Expert (81-100)
Candidate shows deep expertise, mentors others, and can discuss nuanced trade-offs and advanced scenarios.

## Interview Red Flags
- Cannot provide specific examples (only theoretical knowledge)
- Takes credit for team work without clarifying individual contribution
- Blames others for failures without showing self-reflection
- Cannot explain WHY they made certain decisions
- Lacks curiosity — doesn't ask questions about the role or company
- Inconsistencies between resume claims and interview responses
- Unable to discuss failures or learning moments
